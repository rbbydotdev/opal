# French Onion Soup Recipe

---

## Description

French Onion Soup is a **classic French dish** made with caramelized onions, 
beef broth, and topped with toasted bread and melted cheese.  
It’s rich, comforting, and perfect for a cozy meal.

> Fun fact: French Onion Soup dates back to **18th-century France** and was 
> considered a dish for the poor because onions were cheap and plentiful.

---

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients
- 4 large **yellow onions**, thinly sliced
- 3 tbsp **unsalted butter**
- 2 tbsp **olive oil**
- 1 tsp **sugar**
- 1 tsp **salt**
- 2 cloves **garlic**, minced
- 8 cups **beef stock**
- 1/2 cup **dry white wine**
- 2 sprigs **fresh thyme**
- 1 **bay leaf**
- Freshly ground **black pepper**

### For the Topping
1. Baguette slices (toasted)  
2. Gruyère cheese (grated)  
3. Parmesan cheese (optional)  

---

## Instructions

1. **Caramelize the onions**  
   - Heat butter and olive oil in a large pot.  
   - Add onions, sugar, and salt.  
   - Cook on medium-low heat for *30–40 minutes* until golden brown.  

2. **Deglaze the pot**  
   - Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.  
   - Pour in white wine and scrape the bottom of the pot.  

3. **Simmer the soup**  
   - Add beef stock, thyme, and bay leaf.  
   - Simmer uncovered for 30 minutes.  
   - Season with salt and pepper.  

4. **Prepare the topping**  
   - Toast baguette slices.  
   - Sprinkle with Gruyère cheese.  

5. **Assemble and serve**  
   - Ladle soup into oven-safe bowls.  
   - Place toasted bread on top.  
   - Sprinkle with more cheese.  
   - Broil until cheese is melted and bubbly.

## Tips & Variations

- Use **vegetable stock** for a vegetarian version.  
- Try **Swiss cheese** instead of Gruyère.  
- Add a splash of **brandy** for extra depth.
